Glossary

Market Capitalization
The total value of a company's outstanding shares of stock, calculated by multiplying the current market price per share by the total number of outstanding shares.
Index Fund
A type of mutual fund or exchange-traded fund (ETF) that seeks to replicate the performance of a specific market index, such as the S&P 500 or the Dow Jones Industrial Average.
Leverage
The use of borrowed funds to increase the potential return of an investment, amplifying both gains and losses.
Margin Call
A demand from a broker for additional funds or securities to cover potential losses on an investment that has moved against the investor.
Volatility Index (VIX)
A measure of the market's expectation of future volatility based on options pricing, often referred to as the "fear gauge."
